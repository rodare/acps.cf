Intervention 1

Project title: "Constructing Meaning through Agroecological Cultural Areas (AOK): Social Approaches and Environmental Sustainability from a Constructivist Perspective"
By Rodrigo Arenas Catalán
Scientific discipline: Environmental anthropology
Planned duration of the project (in months): 36
Keywords: Sustainable Development, Agroecology, Art, Environmental Art, Environmental Agroecology, International Relations
Aim of the research project: To test the ability of the AOK to stimulate visitors through interventions in the arts and agroecology to exhibit behaviors other than traditional parks, fostering a deeper and richer relationship with nature.
Tool: 3 boxes each with a pencil that function as boxes made by me and volunteers from materials found in Zakrzówek and which allows expression and communication between people
Information collection method: PAPI
Research question: Express yourself in a list
Purpose of the research tool: Open a channel of expression and visual and artistic communication between people on AOK, which can also be used as a message box.
Results: Images stay in the box, but accessible to people who can open it anytime to see what is written or drawn inside. The drawings will be interpreted by interested parties like me and volunteers.
Analysis criteria:

  2. There are images or not?
  3. How many paintings are there?
  4. Was the policy of using the box estimated or not?
  5. As a viewer of the paintings, do I like them or not? How do I feel about them?
  6. Can you see the artistic intention or not, or more specific?
  7. Does it agree with the perspective of other volunteers?
  8. Are there common items in the picture in other boxes? Eg sea and swimming
  • Does the tool allow to answer the formulated research questions?
    • Yes, as an expression channel, the tool allows you to respond to the request: "Express yourself in the list" because the leaves will have drawings made by people for themselves and for other people to see.
  • For which not, for which insufficiently?
    • It doesn't quite answer the main purpose of this research project because you can't deepen your understanding of nature, only in the sense that the box is only made of materials found on site.
  • Does it consist of questions arising from the research problem?
    • Yes, there is only one request: "Express yourself on the list"
  • Are any questions unnecessary/off-topic
    • Are any questions missing? The question, rather the request is open for 2 reasons: 1) There shouldn't be a lot of explanation because it bores people. 2) There is not enough space for a lot of questions on a mailbox.
  • Is the logic of the narrative coherent, continuous, legible?
    • Yes, the instructions are written in charcoal pencil on the tree bark covering the box
  • Are all the questions understandable?
    • Yes, even a child can understand what's going on
  • Are all words and definitions understandable?
    • Yes, the instructions are written in Polish, but thanks to the fact that there are also visual explanations, it can be understood without being dependent on the language
  • Are the selected measurement levels appropriate?
    • Yes, they are written at the top titled: "Analysis Criteria"
  • Does the tool contain all formally necessary elements (introduction, rules of participation, etc.)
    • Yes, the rules of participation will be clearly written on the inbox
  • Is the tool attractive to the recipient?
    • How do people understand that the standing box is not a bird house (since it will bear the college logo, I expect people's interest and therefore a lot of participation.
